History

The recreation area was established in 1932, but mainly as a small safari park holding native
and exotic animals. Over the years, it started to attract more visitors, which resulted in area
expansion. Currently covering 22 hectares in the North of Marselisborg Forest, the deer park is
still maintained fully by volunteers from Aarhus Community.
Nowadays, Dyrehaven is occupied by only two deer species: roughly thirty sika and forty roe
deer.

Ideal way to spend time

Aarhus’ Deer Park is beautiful year-round, and perfect for any type of visit. Bring your grandma
to feed the deer, have a picnic date at one of the many benches, or take your personal
photographer with you for instagram sessions. If distance or cold weather is holding you back
for the time being, you could always watch some youtube videos instead:)
